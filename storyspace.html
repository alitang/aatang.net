Story Space

responsive website

An interactive, location-based story sharing website that increases exposure to local and global social issues. Story Space aims to create a culture of compassion for people of different backgrounds. Created in collaboration with designer Tiffany Choi and computer engineer Kelvin Lu.

Brainstorm

In preparation for this project, Tiffany and I researched websites of similar intents to gather ideas on how to build empathy. We came across many effective story-sharing blogs, but none of which included any interactive aspects. From our research, we decided to include location as a factor on our platform to help users feel more connected to the stories.

Preliminary Prototypes

For our first iteration, we created a paper prototype to test the navigation on users. It became apparent that one of the major problems with our platform would be the navigation; we would need to create a system that is intuitive to users.

From the feedback, we created a digital mockup and then a preliminary prototype. We focused our attention on the top bar, adding colors to separate information and short instructions to teach users how to use the site.

Check out the prototype for yourself by clicking on the button below!

Feedback into Final Prototype

Our second round of user testing raised the concern for privacy. Many users were concerned with publicizing their location as they were also sharing sensitive stories. Tiffany and I decided to create a list view, which would allow users to post stories without sharing their location. The changes are implemented in our final mockup shown above.

For our final iteration, we also created a mobile version of our platform. The major challenge with the responsiveness was consolidating the heavy navigation into a smaller space.
