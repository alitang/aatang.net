Coffee in Davis

responsive website

An informational website introducing the local cafe options in the city of Davis. Coffee in Davis utilizes minimal text and large images to help students find their go-to study or hangout spot.

Brainstorm and Sketches

While I was a student at UC Davis, I visited many coffee shops and lounges in the town to try and find the perfect study environment. After visiting cafe after cafe that wasn't the right fit, I began to wish there was a resource that could tell me what I wanted to know about a cafe without actually having to go there. I knew Yelp was a popular resource, but I didn't want to sift through hundred of comments to find the information I needed. So, using the notes I had personally gathered through my years at UCD, I decided to create "Coffee in Davis".

Preliminary Prototype

My intention for the first iteration was to create a platform that was not only informative, but also visually engaging. I went to each coffee shop and took photos of the space to use as the primary visuals. Once I put all the images together, however, I realized that the interface became cluttered. For my second and final iteration (shown above), I added more white space to separate the information.
