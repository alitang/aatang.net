Edna E. and the Earthquake

responsive website

A children's short story about earthquakes. Narrator Edna E. takes her readers through a "behind the scenes" of an earthquake. Accessible on all digital devices.

Research

In preparing to write the story, I researched the science behind earthquakes and gathered some notes on important points I wanted to deliver. I divided my research into three chapters, "What is happening", "What to do" and "What now", and consolidated the information into short sentences that would become Edna's narration.

Sketches

Once the script had been developed, I played around with sketches to develop the look for Edna. My intention was to create visuals that were simple, colorful and engaging for a young audience. It was a fun challenge illustrating different caricatures. After creating Edna digitally, I moved into sketching storyboards for each chapter. The resulting process ended up being a lot of digital experimentation, playing with colors and layouts to create the mood I was looking for.
